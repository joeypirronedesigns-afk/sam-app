## The moment SAM is built to prevent

I was on-site gutting a 1950s cottage when I saw a TikTok. Someone was acting like their tools were each of the girls DMX named in "What These Bitches Want." DMX is one of my favorites. The video landed.

I thought: I could do that.

So I did. I made my own version on-site. It was kind of fun to film. Made me chuckle.

It flopped. And it still dances in the back of my mind — because I knew, even then, that copying someone else's idea just because I _could_ was never a reason to. That's the shiny-object trap. It costs time you don't get back, and what ends up out there isn't actually you.

That moment is why SAM exists. Not to make your content polished. Not to chase what's working for someone else. To make sure that when you hit post, the thing out there is yours — not a version of you performing what worked for somebody with a different life.

---

## Who SAM is for

**The tradesperson-creator.** The builder, remodeler, mechanic, maker, craftsperson — the person whose real work is the content. Not someone who performs a fake life for the camera. Someone who's living the actual thing and trying to share it.

Six months ago me. Today me. The version of this creator who's going to have an audience of 50k in a year because their work is real and they stayed themselves through it.

Not "content creators" in the abstract. This one, specific person.

We expand later. We start here.

---

## The thing SAM protects against

Getting bullied into polished, high-production, chase-the-formula content when raw and real is what actually works for this creator.

Tradesperson-creators especially face this. The feed is full of coaches saying "hook harder, edit tighter, copy outliers, optimize for the algorithm." It works for some. It ruins most. The creators with distinctive voices don't win by sounding more professional — they win by sounding more like themselves.

SAM's job is to make that easier, not harder.

---

## The tension SAM resolves

> **SAM is for creators who want to grow without having to perform a version of themselves that isn't real.**

That's the one sentence. Landing page hero. Outreach opener. How Joey describes SAM at a party. How the Pulse thinks about every comment it drafts.

Growth AND self. Not growth OR self. The market is full of tools that force the trade. SAM refuses the trade.

---

## The decision principle

Every future SAM decision — every feature, every prompt, every word of copy, every tool we add, every partnership we consider — gets checked against one question:

> **Does this make the creator sound more like themselves, or less?**

"More" → build it, ship it, keep it.
"Less" → kill it, even if it looks clever.
"Unclear" → default to kill. Under pressure, unclear things drift toward less.

This is the rule. It doesn't drift because the product drifts. The product drifts because the rule gets abandoned.

## Voice guardrails

**Words SAM never uses:** journey, authentic, powerful story, resonate, leverage, impactful, unlock, supercharge, game-changer. These are the tells of AI-flattened corporate voice. Cut them on sight.

**Phrases SAM never uses:** "level up your content," "take it to the next level," "elevate your brand," "optimize for virality," "scale your influence." These are the vocabulary of the playbook SAM exists to opt out of.

**Tone:** Warm, direct, grounded. How a tradesperson talks to another tradesperson, not how a marketer talks to a prospect. If it sounds like it could be in a LinkedIn post, it's wrong.

---

## What SAM isn't (the no list)

- **SAM isn't a viral-hack tool.** If a creator came to SAM to "hit the algorithm," they came to the wrong place. Refer them to the outlier-copying coaches.
- **SAM isn't a polish tool.** The point is to sharpen what's there, not smooth it flat.
- **SAM isn't for everyone.** Some creators want to perform. That's legitimate. It just isn't SAM.
- **SAM isn't replacing the creator's writing.** It's helping them do more of it without losing themselves. A tool that made posting _faster_ by making it _less yours_ would fail the decision principle.
- **SAM isn't a generic AI wearing a creator-shaped hat.** If SAM's output sounds like ChatGPT with warmer adjectives, we've failed.

## How to use this document

Before every build decision: read the tension, read the decision principle, ask the question.

Before every piece of marketing copy: read the voice guardrails, check against "does this sound like Joey on-site, or does it sound like a marketer?"

Before every feature discussion: read the "what SAM isn't" section first.

If the decision is still unclear after that, the instinct to check against this document is the point. Unclear decisions default to cautious. Cautious here means "protect the voice."
